<p><p>Refrigerated storage encompasses two main types: chilled storage and frozen storage. Chilled storage maintains temperatures typically between 0°C and 8°C, preserving perishable items like dairy, fruits, and vegetables for short to medium durations. In contrast, frozen storage operates at temperatures below -18°C, ensuring long-term preservation of items such as meat, seafood, and frozen ready meals. Both storage types play vital roles in the supply chain, helping to reduce spoilage, extend shelf life, and maintain food safety standards.</p></p>
