<p><p>The retail video connectivity cable market encompasses various types of cables essential for connecting devices to display technology. VGA (Video Graphics Array) cables, primarily used for older monitors and projectors, transmit analog signals. DVI (Digital Visual Interface) cables support both digital and analog formats, ensuring compatibility with various devices while providing improved quality. HDMI (High-Definition Multimedia Interface) cables dominate modern connections, delivering high-definition video and audio through a single cable, making them ideal for televisions, gaming consoles, and multimedia devices.</p></p>
