Each wavelength serves distinct applications in spectroscopy, influencing the probe's sensitivity and suitability for various materials. The 532nm probe is often used for biological samples, while 633nm is favored for some semiconductor analyses. The 785nm wavelength allows deeper penetration and reduced fluorescence, making it ideal for complex samples, whereas 1064nm probes minimize water interference, enhancing analysis in aqueous solutions. Other wavelengths cater to specialized requirements.</p></p>
