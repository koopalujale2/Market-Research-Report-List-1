<p><strong>The OBD Interface Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>OBD-I</li><li>OBD-II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The OBD interface market primarily consists of two types: OBD-I and OBD-II. OBD-I, introduced in the 1980s, provided limited diagnostic capabilities, varying by manufacturer, and was primarily used for emissions control. In contrast, OBD-II, standardized in the mid-1990s, offers comprehensive diagnostic features, including real-time data and standardized trouble codes. OBD-II supports a wider range of vehicles, enhancing diagnostics and emissions monitoring, making it the dominant standard in modern automotive technology.</p></p>
